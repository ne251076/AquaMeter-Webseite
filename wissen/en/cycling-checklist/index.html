Why the order matters

Cycling happens in two consecutive waves: first, bacteria that break down ammonia establish themselves, then — usually with a delay — the ones that break down the resulting nitrite into nitrate. That delay is exactly what causes the dreaded "nitrite peak" around week 2–4, often right when beginners have started feeling safe because ammonia has already dropped to zero.

This checklist is a guideline, not a rigid schedule. Depending on tank size, starter bacteria, and water temperature, cycling can take shorter or longer. Test regularly and go by your actual readings, not the calendar.

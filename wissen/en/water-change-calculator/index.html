How is this calculated?

The formula is based on simple dilution math: when part of your tank water is replaced with fresh water (usually with a lower or zero level of the substance in question), the concentration drops proportionally to the amount changed. The exact formula: Fraction to change = (Current reading − Target reading) ÷ (Current reading − Tap water reading).

Important: This calculation assumes a one-time, instant dilution. In practice, fish waste and leftover food keep producing new nitrate/phosphate continuously — the actual reading a few days later will usually be somewhat higher than what's calculated right after the water change.

Practical note on large water changes

At very high starting values, the math can call for a water change well above 50% to hit your target in one step. For changes that large, caution is warranted: split it into two smaller changes over 2–3 days instead of doing it all at once — this is easier on fish and plants, which would otherwise have to adapt abruptly to sharply changed water parameters.

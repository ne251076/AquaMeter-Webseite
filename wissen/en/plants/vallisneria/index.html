Vallisneria (Vallisneria spiralis)

Vallisneria is a substrate-rooted background plant with a genuine superpower: it thrives in hard, mineral-rich water — unlike most other aquarium plants.

Originwidespread, tropical to subtropical
Typesubstrate-rooted runner plant, background
Lightmoderate, 7–8 hours daily
CO2not required — uses bicarbonate
pH leveltolerates up to 8.5 (hard water)
Carbonate hardness (KH)tolerates high values well
Propagationvia runners
Difficultyeasy

The superpower: suited for hard water

Unlike most aquarium plants, Vallisneria can use bicarbonate as a carbon source instead of relying on free CO2. That makes it an ideal choice for tanks with high carbonate hardness and high pH — such as Malawi cichlid tanks — where most other plant species struggle.

Planting and propagation

Unlike epiphytes such as Anubias, Vallisneria is planted with its roots in the substrate. Over time it produces strong runners and spreads on its own into a dense "meadow" in the background.

Tip for cichlid tanks: Digging fish species can easily uproot freshly planted Vallisneria. A ring of gravel around the planting spot or using larger starter plants helps until it anchors itself properly.

Light

Moderate light for 7 to 8 hours daily is enough. Overly intense lighting can encourage hair algae, especially in hard water — it's worth keeping an eye on both duration and intensity.

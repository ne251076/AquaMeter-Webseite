Neon Tetra (Paracheirodon innesi)

The neon tetra is one of the best-selling aquarium fish worldwide — yet keeping them well is a bit more demanding than their beginner reputation suggests.

OriginAmazon basin (Peru, Colombia, Brazil)
Size1.4–1.6 in
Tank sizemin. 32 in / 80 cm footprint
Social structureSchooling fish, at least 10 individuals
Temperature68–75°F (up to 79°F possible)
pH level5.0–7.0
General hardness (GH)6–15 °dGH
Lifespanseveral years with good care
DietOmnivore: fine flake food, occasional frozen/live food

Water parameters in detail

Neon tetras need soft, slightly acidic water — incorrect water parameters make them noticeably more prone to disease. Warmer water (up to 79-81°F) is possible but raises metabolic activity and can shorten lifespan. More on the basics in our pH Level and GH articles.

Tank mates

Good fit

  • Other South American species with similar water needs
  • Small corydoras, otocinclus
  • Bristlenose pleco

Not suitable

  • Angelfish (predation risk — neons fit their mouth)
  • Larger predatory fish

Notable traits

Neon tetras are known for being prone to ich (white spot disease) when moved to a new tank — watch them closely, especially in the first few weeks.

Breeding in a normal community tank is practically impossible — it requires a separate breeding tank with very specific, soft and acidic water conditions.

Common mistakes

The most common beginner mistakes: too small a school (under 10 fish causes stress), water that's too hard, and housing them with angelfish, which may eventually view neons as prey.
